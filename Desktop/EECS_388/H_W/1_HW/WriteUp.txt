1E - How is this similar to the Kasiski attack?
		Explain using the mean of the frequency variances that you calculated in this
part and part (d).
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The Kasiski attack is preformed by first finding the length of the key. To do this,
an attacker finds repeat sequences, length 3 or more, and determines the gap
between. Possible key lengths are determined by taking the factors of these
gaps/spacing.

In part D and E, we split a cypherText and key into individual sets
corresponding to the index from an assumed Key Length and determine the plainText
population variance by averaging the individual variances from each set.

Similarities arise in the way the two methods guess the key length. For Kasiski,
once the attacker has determined the possible key lengths from assessing factors of
the repeated sequence spacing length, it assumes each possible length and preforms
the same analysis as we did in D and E to determine the proper Key Length.

For both methods, the proper key length is the one which produces a population
variance most similar to the language in which the plaintext was written.

Briefly, what is “snakeoil cryptography”? How does it relate to the exercise above?

Snake oil cryptography is the concept of cryptographic systems or techniques that
make unrealistic claims about their security. Snake oil was historically viewed as
a "miracle cure" even though it was just a sham. This term draws parallels between a that "miracle cure" and a
cryptographic system that is marketed as secure where in fact the system or technique is vulnerable.

Vigenere cipher was once known as "the unbreakable cipher" which turns out to be
a version of Snake oil cryptography as the program we write in this homework
can break it.
